My Recommendation

Palette 1: Terracotta & Sage is the strongest choice for Luma.

  • Most distinctive: Terracotta is uncommon in SaaS — it immediately differentiates from blue/purple/teal competitors
  • Human & approachable: Pottery clay signals handcraft and human work — perfect for "invisible work made visible"
  • Aligns with Chrissy Elle: Warm, neutral, understated — matches her brand's warmth without copying
  • Works with existing design: Plus Jakarta Sans + JetBrains Mono feel even better with earth tones than with indigo
  • Sage green success color: Natural growth metaphor, calmer than bright green, still readable
  • Premium without pretension: Terracotta feels crafted and intentional, not flashy

Palette 2 (Clay & Olive) is a close second if you want more sophistication and less vibrancy. Palette 3 (Rust) has the most energy but may feel too bold. Palette 4 (Coffee) is too subdued — risks feeling generic brown.
